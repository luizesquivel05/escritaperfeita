VIVARA IN THE MARA COLONY


   Vivara in the Mara Colony is a literary work that aims to present the central idea -> to talk about Literary Theory in a simple, objective, and accessible way to all audiences.

   Through this work, it becomes public that life and all work are within literary theory, criticizing its attempts at objectification and subordination that academies impose against theories; it is, above all, a critical work.

   If literary theory is meant to study literature, then why should we confine ourselves within our libraries and academies? Literary theory must overcome barriers, discuss literary themes. Hunger, thirst, human misery, inequality... These are human and literary themes. How could we conceive of a literary theory that would distance itself from them?

   In an objective and synthesized way, the work is situated in a context where Vivara's world is living normally, without major events or milestones: we are not in crisis or wars, but we are also not in great moments of growth or proliferation.

   At this moment, there is a Canaanite, just one among the many Canaanites who live on the margins of society, forgotten and ignored. His name is Lucexander, and throughout the chapters, he travels through Vivara, getting to know each of the nacaroes and seeking answers to his many questions.

Discover a world full of complexities, current events, and questions in "Vivara Speaking of Mara." In this engaging literary work, we are invited to explore Vivara through the unique perspective of Lucexander, a literature enthusiast who relentlessly seeks true happiness.
 
Through the pages of this book, we are transported to Vivara, a place that exists in a moment of relative stability, without major crises or advancements. However, it is in this seemingly ordinary setting that Lucexander, a forgotten and marginalized Canaanite, embarks on a journey of self-dis covery. Join him as he travels through Vivara, unraveling secrets, encountering different characters, and seeking answers to his countless questions.
 
"Vivara Speaking of Mara" goes beyond exploring the literary world. It transcends the boundaries of academia and libraries, delving into the depths of literary theory to address universal and human themes. Hunger, thirst, misery, and inequality are some of the issues that permeate Lucexander's reflections, raising important questions about the relationship between literary theory and the reality experienced by all of us.
 
Written with clarity and objectivity, this book aims to present literary theory in an accessible way to all audiences. You don't need to be a literature expert to be enchanted by the engaging narrative and profound reflections that permeate each page. "Vivara Speaking of Mara" is a critical work that challenges attempts at objectification and subordination imposed by academia, inviting us to reflect on the transformative power of literature.
